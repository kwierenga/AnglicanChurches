## Summary
St. Thomas Anglican Church serves the small town of Lacovia in the parish of St. Elizabeth. It is one of several historic Anglican churches in Jamaica's southern lowlands.

## History
Lacovia was once a more prominent settlement, located along the Black River which served as a major trade route for sugar and logwood in the eighteenth and nineteenth centuries. An Anglican church was established here to serve the local population during the colonial era.

The church has continued to serve the Lacovia community through Emancipation, Independence, and into the present day. St. Elizabeth is one of Jamaica's largest and most rural parishes, and churches like St. Thomas have played an important role in community life.

## Structure/Architecture
The church is a modest stone building suited to a small-town congregation. It features a simple nave-and-chancel plan with a pitched roof. The building reflects the practical, sturdy construction common to rural Jamaican churches built to withstand hurricanes and earthquakes.

## Worship & Clergy
Regular Anglican services are held for the local congregation. The church is part of the wider network of Anglican parishes in St. Elizabeth.

## Interesting Facts
- Lacovia sits on the Black River, which was historically one of Jamaica's most important commercial waterways.
- St. Elizabeth is known as the "breadbasket" of Jamaica for its agricultural output.
- The parish contains several other historic Anglican sites, including ruins and chapels.
