## Summary
The Cathedral of St. Jago de la Vega in Spanish Town is one of the oldest Anglican cathedrals in the Western Hemisphere. It has served as the mother church of the Anglican Diocese of Jamaica since the seventeenth century.

## History
A place of Christian worship has stood on this site since the Spanish established Santiago de la Vega as the island's capital in 1525. When the English captured Jamaica in 1655, the former Spanish chapel was converted for Anglican use. The first brick church on the site was erected in the 1660s under English colonial rule.

The present cathedral dates largely from a major rebuilding in 1714, with further restorations over the centuries following hurricane and earthquake damage. Spanish Town served as Jamaica's capital until 1872, and the cathedral was at the centre of civic and religious life throughout that period. Governors, legislators, and military officers worshipped here, and many are commemorated inside.

After the capital moved to Kingston, the cathedral continued as the seat of the diocese. It remains an active place of worship and a monument to nearly five centuries of Christian presence on the site.

## Structure/Architecture
The cathedral is built of brick and stone in a simple Georgian style, with a pitched timber roof and a square western tower added in the nineteenth century. The interior features a traditional nave-and-chancel plan with side aisles.

Notable features include numerous memorial tablets on the walls, commemorating colonial-era figures, and a collection of historic silver plate. The building has been repaired and restored many times, most recently to address hurricane damage and general wear.

## Worship & Clergy
Regular Anglican services are held, including Sunday Eucharist and special feast-day celebrations. The cathedral is served by a dean and chapter as part of the Diocese of Jamaica and the Cayman Islands. It also hosts civic and diocesan events.

## Interesting Facts
- The site has been in continuous use for Christian worship since the early sixteenth century.
- The cathedral contains some of the oldest memorial monuments in the English-speaking Caribbean.
- Spanish Town's central square, adjacent to the cathedral, is itself a National Heritage site featuring the Rodney Memorial and the Old King's House.
