## Summary
Treasure Beach Mission Chapel is a small Anglican chapel serving the coastal community of Treasure Beach in south St. Elizabeth. It reflects the tradition of mission chapels established to serve remote settlements.

## History
Treasure Beach is a collection of fishing villages along Jamaica's quiet south coast. Unlike the busy north-coast resort towns, this area developed slowly and retained a close-knit community character. The mission chapel was established to bring Anglican worship to a settlement too far from the main parish church for regular attendance.

Mission chapels like this one were common across rural Jamaica, serving as both places of worship and community gathering points. The chapel continues to serve the Treasure Beach community alongside the area's growth as an eco-tourism destination.

## Structure/Architecture
The chapel is a small, simple building in keeping with its mission-chapel origins. It has a modest nave without the aisles or tower found in larger parish churches. The design is functional and suited to the tropical climate, with good ventilation and a durable roof.

## Worship & Clergy
Services are held periodically, often led by clergy who also serve other churches in the area. The chapel hosts worship, community events, and seasonal celebrations.

## Interesting Facts
- Treasure Beach is known for its distinctive community of mixed heritage, including Scottish, African, and Taino ancestry.
- The area hosts the annual Calabash International Literary Festival.
- Treasure Beach is one of Jamaica's leading community-based tourism destinations.
