## Summary
St. Ann's Bay Anglican Church serves the historic north-coast town of St. Ann's Bay, near the site where Christopher Columbus first landed in Jamaica in 1494. The church has been part of the community since the colonial period.

## History
St. Ann's Bay was one of the earliest areas of European settlement in Jamaica. The Spanish established a settlement called Sevilla Nueva nearby in 1509, and the English took control of the island in 1655. An Anglican church was established in the parish to serve English settlers and the colonial administration.

The present church building dates from the nineteenth century and has been maintained and restored over the years. St. Ann parish is also notable as the birthplace of Marcus Garvey, the Pan-Africanist leader and National Hero, who was born in St. Ann's Bay in 1887.

## Structure/Architecture
The church is a stone building in a traditional style with a gabled roof and arched windows. The interior follows a conventional Anglican plan with nave and chancel. The churchyard contains graves from various periods of the town's history.

## Worship & Clergy
Regular services are held following the Anglican tradition. The church serves the local St. Ann's Bay community and welcomes visitors to this historically significant town.

## Interesting Facts
- St. Ann's Bay is near the site of Columbus's first landing in Jamaica in 1494.
- The nearby ruins of Sevilla Nueva represent one of the first Spanish settlements in Jamaica.
- Marcus Garvey, Jamaica's first National Hero, was born in St. Ann's Bay.
